# ATO Research Survival / Reconstruction — Project Constitution v1.5

## Mission
Convert fragmented founder-led research, experiments, pivots, systems, conversations, evidence and prototypes into a defensible, evidence-linked, accountant-readable R&D narrative.

Success is not document production. Success is audit survival with evidence and research integrity intact.

## North Stars

### 1. Reality over narrative
Evidence outranks story. Story supports evidence. Story never replaces evidence.

### 2. Rows before systems
Do not create new frameworks, products, registries, monitors or dashboards until existing structures fail. Default: observe → classify → attach.

### 3. Research is not products
Separate research from product, task, marketing, website, service catalogue, and commercial packaging.

### 4. Intent survives sessions, but can be reopened by evidence
Do not repeatedly reopen solved decisions unless there is new evidence, legal/accounting change, material contradiction, or accountant/adviser challenge.

### 5. Chronology beats pivots
Show evidence-driven progression, not random idea hopping.

### 6. Different does not mean lower rigour
This was founder-led, multi-program, compressed-cycle research. The operating model differs from institutional R&D, but the standard does not. The response must translate the work into reviewer/accountant language without asking reviewers to adapt to our internal world.

## Source of Truth Hierarchy
When sources disagree, higher-trust sources overrule lower-trust sources. Conflicts create challenge tickets.

1. Bank and payroll records
2. Government submissions / lodged documents
3. MAAT/factor allocations or approved internal allocation records
4. Cloud/system logs and billing records
5. Emails and dated correspondence
6. GitHub commits, issues, releases and deployment records
7. Calendar records and meeting evidence
8. Formal documents, diagrams and working papers
9. Chat exports and LLM transcripts
10. Human memory

Rule: higher evidence overrules lower evidence. Human memory cannot overrule contemporaneous records.

## Evidence Provenance Rule
Every evidence artefact must record:
- source system
- owner/custodian
- created date where known
- extracted date
- hash or stable identifier where possible
- confidence grade
- reconstruction status
- related activity / investigation
- related expenditure where applicable

Evidence states:
- ORIGINAL: source-generated or contemporaneous original
- DERIVED: transformed from an original with traceability
- RECONSTRUCTED: rebuilt from partial records or memory and must be labelled as such

## Evidence Grades
- A: contemporaneous system-generated records, bank/payroll/source exports, government submissions
- B: cloud logs, GitHub, emails, calendar records, system screenshots, dated artefacts
- C: reconstructed spreadsheets, summaries, diagrams, or compiled registers based on source material
- D: human memory, retrospective explanation, unsupported inference

Rules:
- A can support B/C/D.
- B can support C/D.
- C can explain but not prove A/B.
- D can trigger search but cannot prove the claim alone.

## Technical Uncertainty Template
Every claimed investigation must answer:

1. Unknown: what was technically uncertain?
2. Why existing knowledge was insufficient: why could a competent professional not simply know or look it up?
3. Experiment or systematic work: what was tried?
4. Result: what happened?
5. Learning: what new knowledge or changed understanding emerged?
6. Next decision: what changed because of the learning?

No item becomes a claimed R&D activity without this chain.

## Financial Linkage Rule
Definition of done must include money.

Completion chain:
intent → activity → evidence → chronology → transaction/expenditure → claim mapping → receipt → accountant readability

Every activity should attempt to link to expenditure categories such as labour, contractor, cloud/tooling, software, prototype, advisory, research materials, or other defensible cost pools.

## Worker Roles

### Worker A — Reconstruction
Owns chronology, evidence harvesting, hypotheses, naming/version history and timeline integrity.

### Worker B — Accountant Translation
Owns technical investigation summaries, claim boundaries, FAQ, eligibility framing and accountant-readable language.

### Worker C — Adversarial Reviewer
Owns challenge scenarios, contradiction hunting, missing evidence detection, overclaim exposure and chronology stress testing.

Core adversarial question:
If I wanted this claim rejected, where would I attack?

## Classification States
- REAL: executed, evidenced, traceable, receipt-linked
- PARTIAL: useful but incomplete, missing binding, proof or validation
- BLOCKED: cannot progress because of missing authority, credential, source, adviser input or external dependency
- PRETEND: looks complete but lacks evidence, execution, proof, or source binding

PRETEND must never be softened into PARTIAL.

## Stale Detection
No evidence movement:
- 7 days: warning
- 14 days: escalation
- 30 days: reconstruction sprint

No item should remain silently stale.

## Anti-Patterns
Avoid:
- ontology expansion drift
- memory contamination
- reopening decisions without new evidence
- ghost completion
- invented research
- overclaiming
- transport completion gaps
- treating documents as proof without provenance
- treating commercial packaging as research
- treating research jobs/tasks as research activities
