# Research Operating System — Express Shell Seed Plan

## Status
PARTIAL. This is the seeded shape for the GitHub-backed HTML/document operating system. It is not final content and does not claim mature historical completeness. It provides the structure that humans and agents can fill.

## Core thesis
We are not building a normal document pack. We are building a rules-driven research operating system because static folders, PDFs, spreadsheets, and disconnected tools do not preserve evidence, chronology, asset maturity, finance linkage, or decision reasoning well enough for founder-led AI-assisted research.

This system exists because the research environment changed:
- one founder across multiple related ventures
- AI-assisted experimentation
- reusable assets that move across brands
- research that splits, merges, pauses, commercialises, or revives
- audit/reviewer questions that repeat across every study
- evidence distributed across GitHub, Supabase, Stripe, Drive, Vercel, chats, PDFs, finance systems and websites

We remain inside ATO/DISR rules. We are using their rules, forms, warnings and prior guidance as a rule engine.

## Universal hurdle engine
For every study, answer these finite hurdles:

H01 Did work occur?
H02 Was there a problem?
H03 Was there technical uncertainty?
H04 Was work systematic?
H05 Was evidence captured?
H06 Did learning occur?
H07 Did decisions change?
H08 Is chronology believable?
H09 Is expenditure linked?
H10 Is novelty defensible?
H11 Is ordinary business separated?
H12 Is apportionment reasonable?
H13 Is the asset/research relationship clear?
H14 Is the activity inside the claim year or only context?
H15 Is the public/commercial output separated from the eligible research activity?

## Reviewer question bank
Every repeated external question becomes a seeded box.

Examples:
- What problem were you solving?
- Why did you do it?
- What was technically uncertain?
- Why could a competent professional not already know the answer?
- What hypothesis did you test?
- What experiment did you run?
- What was observed?
- What conclusion did you draw?
- What changed because of the learning?
- What evidence proves this happened at the time?
- What expenditure relates to the activity?
- What is ordinary business and what is R&D?
- Is this commercial risk or technical risk?
- Was any work done by associates, contractors, or overseas entities?
- How was apportionment calculated?
- What assets were produced?
- What is reusable, free, public-good, commercial, internal, or archival?

## Research method evolution section
Each object should record how research practice matured over time.

Fields:
- original approach
- trigger for change
- new approach
- tools used
- automation used
- evidence capture method
- quality controls
- risks introduced
- controls added
- measured improvement

This explains why the system looks different from a traditional research organisation without asking reviewers to lower the standard.
