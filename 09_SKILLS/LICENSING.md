## Why Apache-2.0 (not MIT, not CC-BY-NC)

| Concern | Apache-2.0 | MIT | CC-BY-NC 4.0 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commercial use (RDTI, Westpac, ConsentX, RETAIL) | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ blocks all |
| Patent grant (ConsentX defensibility) | ✅ explicit | ❌ silent | ❌ N/A |
| Contributor licence (downstream T4H modules) | ✅ explicit | ⚠️ implicit | ❌ N/A |
| Compatibility with T4H portfolio | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Reuse by external partners | ✅ with attribution | ✅ with attribution | ❌ NC blocks |

Apache-2.0 wins because of the explicit patent grant. ConsentX positions T4H against potential patent claims around consent-orchestration patterns; a permissive licence with patent grant is the safer foundation.
