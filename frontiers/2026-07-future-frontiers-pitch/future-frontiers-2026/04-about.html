Troy Latter

Founder, Tech 4 Humanity · XRSI APAC Lead

Troy is a technologist, futurist and strategic AI advisor with 15+ years across the world's largest technology firms — Amazon Web Services, Oracle, Unisys, HPE and Gartner — driving sovereign-grade AI, security and infrastructure programs across the Asia-Pacific. He founded Tech 4 Humanity in Sydney as an independent R&D vehicle to study and build technologies that take humans seriously — rather than as data, friction or growth targets.

He is the XRSI APAC Lead (the XR Safety Initiative), a regular keynote speaker, podcaster, and active learner in non-invasive brain-computer interfaces. He has worked at ministerial and C-level on emerging technology ethics, advising government and enterprise on how to anticipate, mitigate and manage the consequences of algorithms, AI and robotics. He holds a Master's degree from UNSW.

His current research programme covers AI Sweet Spots (an empirical study of cognitive workload and AI effectiveness with n = 11,728 validated participants), the Drug Resilience Atlas, ConsentX (consent infrastructure for the AI era), and Doolittles (a cognitive runtime for speech-impaired communication).

Troy speaks in plain English about technical material. He's comfortable in a producer chair, on a panel, or doing the research the producer doesn't have time to do. He doesn't oversell, doesn't hype, and won't repeat what's already on every other show — because there's plenty of genuinely new ground to cover.

Why work with us.

Producer time is the most expensive thing in television. Here's what we'd actually bring to the slot — beyond the talking head.

First-hand, not second-hand

Active operator across these frontiers — not a commentator quoting other people's research. We deploy systems, run studies, and live with the consequences.

Producer pace

Responsive on producer timeframes. Pre-briefs delivered ahead of slot. Talking points clean, sourced, and on the producer's desk before they ask.

Sources you can trust

Claims tied to primary research and verifiable evidence. We'll tell you what we know, what's plausible, and what's hype — without dressing one up as the other.

A network of voices

Across Australian academia, healthcare, defence and industry. If a story needs a panelist or counter-voice, we can usually find one — fast.

Built for clarity, not hype

The mission is amplifying human capability — which only works if the audience actually understands what's happening. Clarity is the point, not the side-effect.

Confidential by default

Pre-broadcast research, contact lists, and background material treated as the producer's. We don't post our pitch decks on the internet — and we don't share what's discussed off-record.

How we'd work together

Four concrete commitments to your slot.

Discovery

One conversation about audience, slot, tone, and which frontier story fits the show — not a pre-canned pitch.

Pre-brief

A two-page document per segment: claim, evidence, the question, the visuals, and three potential follow-ups.

Delivery

In-chair, on-time, audience-aware. We can flex the language to match the slot — popular, technical, or somewhere in between.

Follow-through

Post-segment notes, audience-Q response material, and a heads-up on the next frontier story before it lands elsewhere.
