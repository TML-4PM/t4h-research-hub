## Where the white-label thesis sits in this landscape

The category gap T4H is positioned to address:

> **There is no horizontal pack-matrix substrate that small (1–10 person) operator-businesses can plug into to get pre-priced, RLS-secured, AU-jurisdiction-aware compliance + operations packs across multiple verticals, with consent-wrapped signal escalation and a Customer Centricity Gate keeping technical execution honest.**

That's an explicit claim. Three ways it could be wrong:

1. **Vanta + Drata + Karbon will bundle horizontally.** They might. Their wedge is deep, single-vertical. Horizontal bundling is hard for them — different go-to-market, different procurement. T4H window: 12–18 months.
2. **Celonis + UiPath will move down-market.** Unlikely in 18 months. Their economics require six-figure deals. SME segment isn't worth their effort.
3. **A new entrant takes this space first.** Real risk. The wedge is visible. Speed matters.

## Decisions this implies

1. **Don't pretend uniqueness on the substrate.** Pack matrices exist. The wedge is the horizontal + AU + ConsentX + Customer Gate combination — defend that, don't claim the substrate.
2. **Pick one vertical to dominate in 90 days.** Tradies is the only one with a live landing page. Either double down there or pick a new one with partner distribution.
3. **Treat OneTrust and Drata as eventual acquirers, not partners.** Frame the platform thesis to make T4H attractive to them at month 18, not month 6.
4. **Publish the Customer Centricity Gate methodology.** It's category-defining IP and currently has no external analogue. Publishing it raises T4H's surface area without giving away the runtime.
